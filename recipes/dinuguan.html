Dinuguan

Another Filipino stew, albeit a more eccentric one, is the dark-tinted dinuguan. It’s made by cooking pork and innards in pig’s blood and vinegar. It’s often eaten with puto, a slightly sweet steamed rice cake, which complements its savory taste

Show Recipe

Recipe

  • ½ kg Pork diced
  • ½ Pork innards diced
  • 2 Gloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium Onion, diced
  • 1/4kg Pork liver, diced
  • 2 Tbls Patis
  • 1 Tsp salt
  • 1 Cup vinegar
  • 2 Cups broth
  • 2 Cups pig’s blood
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 Hot banana peppers

    Steps in cooking

  • Step 1 Cover pork innards with water and simmer for 30 minutes. Removed from broth and dice. Save 1-1/2 cup of broth.
  • Step 2 Heat oil and sauté garlic and onions for a minute using a saucepan. Add pork innards, liver , patis , salt. Sauté for 5 min.
  • Step 3 Add vinegar and bring to boil without stirring. Lower heat and simmer uncovered until most of the liquid has evaporated.
  • Step 4 Add broth, and simmer for 10mins. Stir in blood and sugar; cook until thick, stirring continuously to avoid curding.
  • Step 5 Add hot peppers and cook for 5 more minutes.
